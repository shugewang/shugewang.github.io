About Me

As someone with an academic background in Psychology, I have been consistently learning and incorporating technology into my studies: from the programming courses I took as part of my BSc, to the C++ simulation model I worked on as part of my MSc, to using Python and R for data analysis in my PhD. Moreover, I have worked in an interdisciplinary team of researchers from Psychology, Robotics, and Music & Audio Technology, which has made me appreciate the innovation and productivity that teamwork and collaboration could bring to a workplace. As my PhD comes to an end, I've come to the realisation that I want to work in a role that allows me to combine my favourite parts of being a researcher - solving practical problems in creative ways, working with like-minded people, and experimenting with all kinds of different technology.

In addition to my practical experience in Python and R, I've also started learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript through the ClickMCR bootcamp programme (MadLab) and self-guided courses such as freeCodeCamp and the Odin Project, and attending tech meetups such as Python North West, CodeWith and Women Who Code. These have not only reaffirmed but also strengthened my interest in software development and my determination in pursuing a career in the tech industry.

I am currently looking for an apprenticeship in software development, where I can refine and further my skill set through hands-on experience in a collaborative environment.
